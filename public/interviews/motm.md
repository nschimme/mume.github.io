# The Mud Connector Interview

*This interview was done in April 1998 by The Mud Connector when MUME won the Mud of the Month.*

### What does it take to create a multi-user environment that will last?

**Manwe, Imp:**
First, you don't simply create a multi-user environment that will last for as many years as MUME has. I think that a mud in general has to be a living organism in constant development or it will die abruptly. We have attempted to code an engine whose mechanisms are always closer to those of the virtual reality we want our players to live in.

### Does MUME offer a roleplaying environment?

**Maedhros, Architect:**
Certainly we do not deny that player derive a great deal of satisfaction from combat, but if this was all there was to offer, the game would not be MUME. MUME has a wide spectrum of players and tries to cater to all of their tastes: hack-n-slashers will love the war-element; roleplayers will find that we actively encourage In-Character play.

### What helped MUME reach this level of popularity?

**Ressaven, Architect:**
MUME is distinguished by being more "realistic" and self-consistent than most MUDs, by being strongly themed, and by the way player-killing is regulated by the War. The homelands of the different species are far apart and defended strongly by mobs, so a player who wishes to roleplay a hobbit strolling through the peaceful countryside can do so.

### What plans do you have for MUME in the future?

**Manwe, Imp:**
Mainly, let it live. Thus, making MUME evolve further. This goes from removing some dikumud limitations, to enhancing environmental dynamism while keeping in mind playability.

### World & Mudlle

**Dain, Supervisor of mudlle:**
MUME has a real programming language called MUDLLE (MUD Language for Little Extensions). This combined with stability and reloadability allows our "mudllers" to write and test code without crashing the game.

**Meryaten, Supervisor of the world:**
When I first came to MUME back in 1994, what really captured my attention was that here was a place where I could walk around in Tolkien's world. We have over 14500 rooms open spanning more than 150 zones.
